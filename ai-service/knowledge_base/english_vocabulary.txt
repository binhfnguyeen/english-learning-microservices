## CEFR Levels Explained
The Common European Framework of Reference for Languages (CEFR) has six levels:
- A1 (Beginner): Can understand and use familiar everyday expressions. Very basic phrases.
- A2 (Elementary): Can communicate on simple, routine tasks. Familiar topics like shopping, family, work.
- B1 (Intermediate): Can deal with most situations while traveling. Can describe experiences, events, dreams.
- B2 (Upper-Intermediate): Can interact fluently with native speakers without strain. Can produce clear text on a wide range of subjects.
- C1 (Advanced): Can use language flexibly and effectively for social, academic, and professional purposes.
- C2 (Mastery): Can understand virtually everything heard or read. Can express themselves spontaneously, very fluently and precisely.

## Daily Conversation Vocabulary
Greetings: Hello, Hi, Hey, Good morning, Good afternoon, Good evening, How are you?
Farewells: Goodbye, Bye, See you later, Take care, Have a good day.
Expressing Agreement: Yes, Absolutely, Exactly, I agree, That's right, Of course.
Expressing Disagreement: No, I disagree, I don't think so, Actually, I'm not sure about that.
Asking for Clarification: Could you repeat that? What do you mean? Can you explain that further? I don't understand.
Apologizing: I'm sorry, I apologize, Excuse me, My mistake, Pardon me.

## Business English Vocabulary
Meeting: agenda, minutes, chairperson, participants, action items, follow-up.
Presentations: slides, handout, Q&A, outline, summary, key points.
Emails: subject line, attachment, CC, BCC, reply, forward, regards, sincerely.
Negotiations: offer, counteroffer, terms, agreement, compromise, deal.
Common phrases:
- "Let's move on to the next point."
- "Could you elaborate on that?"
- "I'd like to draw your attention to..."
- "In conclusion, we have agreed to..."

## Academic English Vocabulary
Essay structure: introduction, thesis statement, body paragraphs, topic sentence, supporting evidence, conclusion.
Linking words:
- Addition: furthermore, moreover, in addition, also, besides
- Contrast: however, nevertheless, on the other hand, despite, although
- Cause/Effect: therefore, thus, consequently, as a result, because
- Example: for instance, for example, such as, to illustrate
- Conclusion: in conclusion, to summarize, overall, in summary

## Common Phrasal Verbs
bring up: to mention a topic ("She brought up the issue at the meeting.")
call off: to cancel ("The game was called off due to rain.")
find out: to discover ("Did you find out the answer?")
give up: to stop trying ("Don't give up learning English!")
look up: to search for information ("Look up the word in a dictionary.")
put off: to postpone ("He put off the meeting until Friday.")
run out of: to have no more ("We ran out of time.")
set up: to arrange or establish ("They set up a new company.")
turn down: to refuse ("She turned down the job offer.")
work out: to exercise / to solve ("I need to work out more." / "We'll work it out.")

## Prepositions of Time and Place
Time prepositions:
- at: specific times. "at 3pm", "at noon", "at midnight", "at Christmas"
- on: days and dates. "on Monday", "on 15th March", "on my birthday"
- in: months, years, seasons, parts of day. "in January", "in 2020", "in summer", "in the morning"
- for: duration. "for two hours", "for a week"
- since: from a point in time. "since 2015", "since Monday"
- by: deadline. "Finish it by Friday."
- until/till: up to a time. "I'll wait until 6pm."

Place prepositions:
- at: specific location. "at the station", "at school"
- on: surface. "on the table", "on the wall", "on the floor"
- in: enclosed space. "in the room", "in the city", "in the box"
- between: two things. "between the shops"
- among: more than two. "among the students"
- next to/beside: near. "sit next to me"
- in front of / behind: position relative to something

## Numbers, Dates and Measurements
Cardinal numbers: one, two, three...
Ordinal numbers: first, second, third, fourth...fifth...twentieth...thirty-first
Saying years: 1990 = "nineteen ninety", 2005 = "two thousand and five", 2024 = "twenty twenty-four"
Fractions: 1/2 = "a half", 1/3 = "a third", 3/4 = "three quarters"
Percentages: 75% = "seventy-five percent"
Temperature: 37°C = "thirty-seven degrees Celsius"
Measurements: 1.8m = "one point eight metres", 5km = "five kilometres"
