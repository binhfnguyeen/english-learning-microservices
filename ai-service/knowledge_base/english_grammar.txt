## Present Simple Tense
The present simple is used to describe habits, facts, and routines.
Structure: Subject + base verb (add -s/-es for he/she/it)
Examples:
- I study English every day.
- She works at a school.
- Water boils at 100 degrees Celsius.
Signal words: always, usually, often, sometimes, rarely, never, every day/week/month.

## Present Continuous Tense
The present continuous describes actions happening right now or temporary situations.
Structure: Subject + am/is/are + verb-ing
Examples:
- I am learning English now.
- They are playing football at the moment.
- She is staying in Hanoi this week (temporary).
Signal words: now, at the moment, currently, right now, today, this week.

## Past Simple Tense
The past simple is used for completed actions in the past at a specific time.
Structure: Subject + past verb (verb + -ed for regular; irregular verbs change form)
Examples:
- I visited London last year.
- She went to school yesterday.
- He didn't finish his homework.
Signal words: yesterday, last year/week, ago, in 2010, when.

## Past Continuous Tense
The past continuous describes an action that was in progress at a specific time in the past.
Structure: Subject + was/were + verb-ing
Examples:
- I was watching TV at 8pm last night.
- They were playing when it started raining.
Signal words: while, when, at that time, at 8pm yesterday.

## Present Perfect Tense
The present perfect connects the past to the present. It expresses life experience, recent news, or actions with present results.
Structure: Subject + have/has + past participle
Examples:
- I have visited Japan three times.
- She has just finished her homework.
- They haven't eaten yet.
Signal words: just, already, yet, ever, never, since, for, recently.

## Future Simple Tense
Used for decisions made at the moment of speaking, predictions, and promises.
Structure: Subject + will + base verb
Examples:
- I will call you tomorrow.
- It will rain tonight (prediction).
- I'll help you with that (offer).
Signal words: tomorrow, next week/year, soon, in the future.

## Conditional Sentences
Type 0: General truths. If + present simple, present simple. (If water reaches 100°C, it boils.)
Type 1: Real/possible future. If + present simple, will + base verb. (If it rains, I will stay home.)
Type 2: Unreal present/future. If + past simple, would + base verb. (If I had money, I would travel.)
Type 3: Unreal past. If + past perfect, would have + past participle. (If I had studied, I would have passed.)

## Articles: a, an, the
- Use "a" before consonant sounds: a cat, a university (yu-sound).
- Use "an" before vowel sounds: an apple, an hour (silent h).
- Use "the" for specific nouns already known to both speaker and listener.
- No article for general plurals and uncountable nouns: Dogs are loyal. Water is essential.

## Passive Voice
The passive voice focuses on the object receiving the action rather than the subject.
Structure: Object + to be + past participle (+ by + agent, optional)
Examples:
- The book was written by Hemingway.
- English is spoken worldwide.
- The meeting has been cancelled.
Use passive when: the agent is unknown, unimportant, or when we want to emphasize the action/receiver.

## Modal Verbs
Can: ability/permission. "I can swim." / "Can I open the window?"
Could: past ability/polite request. "She could read at age 4." / "Could you help me?"
May/Might: possibility. "It may rain." / "She might be late."
Must: strong obligation/deduction. "You must wear a seatbelt." / "He must be tired."
Should: advice/recommendation. "You should exercise more."
Would: polite request/conditional. "Would you like tea?" / "I would travel if I could."
